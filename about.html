Goals and Motivations

Expectations for my work

When it comes to computer science and interaction design, my real interests lie in the intersection of the two. I find the logic and structure of programming to be compelling and satisfying, and intertwined with the complexity and unpredictability of the average user, there are endless scenarios to consider and potential problems to solve.

These are the challenges that excite me and inspire me to work towards something that pleases as many people involved as possible - including the designers and developers behind the solution.

Expectations for myself

I am passionate about increasing accessibility, bridging gaps, and providing support for the underprivileged. I strongly believe in the need for those in places of privilege to acknowledge the lack of equity in many systems that currently exist in the world and to take responsibility for righting those wrongs.

In everything I do, I plan to hold myself and my peers accountable for our actions. Our impact stems beyond our active efforts to how we work, socialize, and rest. After all, change starts from within.
